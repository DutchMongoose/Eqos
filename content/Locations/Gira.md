# Gira

The old seat of power of the dwarven empire **Kalmur** — now a vassal state of [[Rothas]]. A cultural mixture of Rothas and the old ways. After Rothas drove back the Giran armies and reached Gira itself, an accord of vassalhood was signed. The inhabitants have largely settled into this arrangement, unchanged from ages past.

## Layout
Located within a single dominant mountain in a small range. The **hollowed-out peak** is entirely filled with city infrastructure, stretching from the opened peak at top to mined depths with a lava lake far below.

- **Main entrance** — North-western side, several carriages wide
- **Suspended paths** — A complex web of walkways crossing the mountain interior, suspended from the ceiling by thick cold-iron chains. Daring merchants secure themselves to the chains and set up stalls. Looking up: the carved mountain and skylight. Looking down: the mines and the glowing lava lake.
- **Ruby Palace** — Free-standing within the mountain space, nearly floating. Lava pumped from the lake provides an eerie glow on its metal decorations.
- **Mern Forge** — Uses the molten earth to forge imbued valuables, producing the **marble-like veins** in metal that are coveted throughout Eqos as noble family heirlooms.

## Politics
The elderly ruler **Roktar** watches the city from his palace balcony. His three daughters await his abdication, each with conflicting visions:

| Daughter | Vision |
|----------|--------|
| **Gurwe** (eldest) | Reestablish Gira as the economic center it was pre-Rothas wars |
| **Berderra** (middle) | Independence from Rothas — but lacks vision for actionable steps |
| **Hallana** (youngest) | Major expansion beyond the mountain; holds Rothasian values, diverging from dwarven tradition |
