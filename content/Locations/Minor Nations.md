# Minor Nations of Eqos

---

## Tethon
Remote nation south-west of Omas, capital **Norvos**. Functions on a system of need — all inhabitants provide what they can and take what they need. Very limited military presence, yet unbothered by other nations. Little sense of monetary value; limited knowledge of earthly resources makes it ill-desired to plunder. People worship spirits of the land and voices of the water.

## Reylor
The **cloud nation** — a dense cloud formation hovering over the Oros region, home to **cloud giants**. Giants interact very little with land creatures, dismissing their limited lifespans and disliking their disrespect for nature. Their societal structure is complex and convoluted — outsiders routinely misinterpret the hierarchy and accidentally insult giants.

*Notable resident: [[Golner]] — storm giant, member of the [[Knowing Keepers|Illustrious Five]].*

## Estaria
A warrior nation of many clans (stationary and nomadic), all pledged to the **Tirr**, who lives in **Elmrim**. When a Tirr dies, each clan proposes a candidate — consensus usually takes a week, often shortened by combats of pride. Each clan is self-sufficient (hunting or fishing). Worship a **nameless towering being** of great strength and endless knowledge.

## Eblis
Desert island nation south-east of [[Rothas]]. Capital **Abadi** — a restless city of beauty where plants flourish and fountains are plenty. Must-see: the **Vertical Gardens of Iqa**, where water travels directly upward before flowing back down the gardens. Living in cities is essential — water is scarce in the **Everchanging Fields**, and its creatures are nightmarish. The **Kadim Pass** is an ordeal unto itself.

## Eowuth
A rough, icy, largely unpopulated landscape. Scattered well-protected hamlets endure snowstorms and hail. Cast off in the [[The Divergence|Divergence]], it remains ill-explored with plenty of opportunity for those willing to risk it.
