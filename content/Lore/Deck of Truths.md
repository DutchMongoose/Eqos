# Deck of Truths

A deck of decorated cards — made from any material (wood, paper, or metal) — used by trained **truthsayers** to uncover hidden truths. Roaming truthsayers charge for readings, burning **bluefern** (a rare plant from far corners of the world) whose smoke allows better connection to the astral threads.

The deck itself is not magical — the connection a trained sayer can obtain gives the cards meaning. Those aligned with [[Narja]] receive more accurate readings.

## Mechanics
- Base deck: **20 cards**
- Untrained readers unconsciously add cards, reflecting their own path
- Draw: roll **2d20** — first die = card, second = orientation (1–10 upright, 11–20 inverted)
- Standard reading: **3 cards** = past, present, future

> [!tip] DM Use
> The Deck is well-suited for steering a party — vague but accurate hints toward a question. *See [[DM Tools]].*

## The 20 Cards

| d20 | Card | Upright | Inverted |
|-----|------|---------|----------|
| 01 | **The Raven Queen** — crucified figure seen from behind, ravens circling | Sacrifice, change, release | Fear, choices, delay |
| 02 | **Melora** — path into forest, flower blooming, a hand reaching from behind a tree | Nature, beauty, balance | People, change |
| 03 | **Pelor** — armored figure with sun directly behind head | Justice, clarity | Doubt, dishonesty, unfairness |
| 04 | **Travel** — horse-pulled wagon on a twisting path toward a distant city | Patience, finding meaning | Lack of balance, loss of direction |
| 05 | **Time** — half-full hourglass with broken top, sand falling in | Cause and effect, aging, focus | Destruction, nearing deadline, control |
| 06 | **Magician** — a Keeper working in a laboratory surrounded by alchemical instruments | Willpower, creativity | Illusions, trickery |
| 07 | **Hermit** — old bearded man in a cave while children play above ground | Search for truth, guidance | Loneliness, isolation |
| 08 | **Tower** — Lyria, the Grey Fort, or a stone tower with fire in a window | Disaster, broken pride | Disaster avoided, suffering |
| 09 | **Lovers** — two people, one upright one inverted, touching hands | Family, union | One-mindedness, disharmony |
| 10 | **Preacher** — Temple of Enlightenment at night, light through exit and skylight, Life Tree lit from below | Religion, intuition | Emotions, dependence |
| 11 | **Knight** — knight sleeping against a tree with a cat stuck in the high branches | Strength, control | Coldness, rigidity, rebellion |
| 12 | **Self** — woman before a decorated mirror; her reflection stares at viewer with one side slightly decayed | Beauty, hope, character | Materialism, pride, decay |
| 13 | **Wealth** — hand holding gold and gems, coins falling | Trade, direction, promises | Theft, hoarding, greed |
| 14 | **Power** — empty stone throne with a circle of people surrounding it | Structure, authority | Corruption, deception, emptiness |
| 15 | **Void** — night sky with light circling inward | Stoicism, calmness, hope | Helplessness, inevitability, fear |
| 16 | **Fate** — grassy hill, starry sky, one red star brighter than the rest | Cycles, fate | Bad luck, doubt |
| 17 | **The Fool** — blindfolded person walking toward a hole, about to fall | Innocence, inner calm | Recklessness, unknowing |

| 20 | **Moon** — same landscape at night, moon behind clouds, wildlife hunting | Silence, rest | Confusion, misinterpretation |

## Additional Cards
Cards can be added or removed as the world progresses. Major actors may gain their own card. Existing cards can be altered — e.g., the Travel card could show the party on their own journey, or player characters could begin replacing the figures depicted.
