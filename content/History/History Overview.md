## The Forgers of Creation

Do you know the feeling when you are awake, yet still sleeping? Being able to shape your dream to your very whim? After a unique collision between the elemental planes, six minds entered consciousness. These six were almost like in a dream and from their thoughts a new dimension flowed. They themselves gained physical form and laid eyes on this tiny space of reality.

These beings are now known as the **Forgers of Creation**. While relatively unknown in the grand scheme of history, they were the start. They kept walking the border between dreams and the waking world — from their thoughts the lands extended. Water, plants and mountains, all were added to the empty canvas of the material plane.

The world was peaceful, however it was quiet, so they created life to entertain them while they dreamed. Once they were content, they each retreated to a resting place, from which they would return once they slept properly.

> [!info] Six Forgers
> There are six Forgers of Creation. Their names, resting places, and individual roles in shaping the world are yet to be fully documented. See [[Forgers of Creation]].

## Age of Flame

The world was peaceful and completely in balance — however balance cannot exist without change. From cracks in the ground, powerful scaled creatures rose with a greed unknown to the inhabitants. Flight and devastating breath weapons allowed these beasts to carve out entire empires in this previously peaceful world.

These [[Dragons|dragons]] existed over a large range of colors, each with their own characteristics and focus. Every dragon type subjugated the creatures in their empire, forcing them to pay tribute and serve. Over thousands of years, each emperor slowly manipulated and transformed their subjects — entire civilizations altered to serve the interests of the dragons, creating the races we know today.

> [!example] Race Origins
> - [[Dwarves]] — shaped by dragons that valued treasures such as gold and gems
> - [[Orcs]] — shaped by dragons that valued pure strength

For centuries the dragon emperors fought with other empires and within their own, as treachery and plots were ever-lurking. Some empires reached populations so large that they tried to rebel against their overlords — as expected, most failed and were largely slaughtered. Over the years, many wars took place, leading to diminishing numbers of true full-grown dragons, as their growth spanned many years.

Some tribes were able to fight off their overlord and cause it to retreat to far-off places and caves. Other dragons decided to simply leave and never return, possibly waiting for a better opportunity.

## Age of Divinity

In the ages that followed, many tribes — largely comprised of singular races — split up and grew together with others. This created a migration of mankind and the start of humanoid empires.

With the world now divided into nations, progress and arcane knowledge were at their height. Almost every major city contained permanent portals to other dimensions, with less dangerous dimensional portals publicly available. Many powerful creatures roamed the lands of [[Eqos]] — most serving as protectors of cities or natural areas. Some of these creatures had even started attracting followers, as small cults began deifying them.

Dragons were deemed extinct, and while the [[Phokar]] employed many drakes, all were glad for it.

### The War of Deliverance & The Divergence

From here the tales of history start to differ, as multiple interpretations of the course of events exist. All parties agree that a cataclysmic event took place, centered at the then-city of [[Fior]] — this event is now known as **[[The Divergence]]**.

| Nation / Group | Their Account |
|---|---|
| [[Phokar]] | The dragon god [[Bahamut]] engulfed the world in flames and flew into the sun, causing earthquakes that split the continent |
| [[Ciria]] | The birth of a new star, which broke through the ground and rose as the all-seeing eye |
| Various tribes | The Day of Retribution — ancient spirits purified the world so it could start anew |
| [[Knowing Keepers]] of [[Rothas]] | The Benevolent Gods warred with the [[Banished Ones]] and sealed them away to protect their subjects |
| [[The Fallen]] | All gods were sealed away equally, with no distinction — making them equal parts victim |

> [!warning] Lost Records
> Any documents detailing the events near Fior were lost in the Divergence itself. A civil war is well-documented at the time, but the exact cause remains unknown.

## Divergence and Present

An explosion of light, a rupture of terrain — tearing apart families, devastating cities. The singular continent of Eqos split into many, leaving a path of destruction at the rupture lines. The capital of Fior was reduced to rubble, its population reduced to ash or crushed by debris.

With other nations equally reeling from the disaster, the minor noble house of [[House Rothuss]] traveled to the site of destruction, built a fortress from the ruins, and established an outpost at the **Gates of ...** — the only feasible land entry into the area. From there they built the kingdom of Rothas.

### Present Day — Year 881

> [!danger] Current Crisis
> The new faction of **The Fallen** has revealed themselves to the world. They are destroying the **world anchors** — created by the Knowing Keepers to keep the planes of existence separate, preventing gods and other creatures from entering the material plane.

Otherworldly influences are beginning to seep through the cracks. While manageable for now, significant intrusions could follow.

**Imminent threat:** During the **mid-winter festival**, the [[The Fallen]] will attack the **[[Varrens Anchor]]**, giving Rothas cause to invade Ciria once more.
